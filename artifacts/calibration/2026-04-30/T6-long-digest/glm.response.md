CryoDAQ has evolved from a foundational data acquisition tool into a robust, intelligent system designed for the rigors of multi-day cryogenic experiments. The project's journey has been defined by a relentless focus on safety, a complete modernization of the operator experience, and the introduction of predictive analytics.

Ensuring Unwavering Safety and Reliability
At the core of CryoDAQ is the guarantee that hardware remains safe. We implemented a rigorous SafetyManager featuring a fail-safe state machine that automatically triggers emergency shutdowns if sensor data becomes stale or temperature rates exceed safe thresholds. Recent releases closed a critical chapter by resolving a subtle communication bug, dubbed the ZMQ idle-death, that could sever the link between the operator interface and the engine. We also extended the timeout for critical safety commands, ensuring that emergency stops are never prematurely canceled during hardware faults. These fixes, alongside atomic database writes and fail-closed configurations, ensure that data integrity and equipment protection are never compromised. The updated alarm engine now supports sophisticated multi-tier alerting, allowing operators to acknowledge warnings and track fault histories confidently.

Modernizing the Operator Experience
We completely overhauled the user interface, replacing the legacy tab layout with a dynamic, theme-aware dashboard. This new shell prioritizes ambient awareness during long experiments, featuring a persistent context strip for critical metrics and streamlined overlays for detailed control. Operators can now choose from twelve visual themes, including light and dark modes, carefully designed to separate interactive elements from safety-critical status indicators. Crucial workflows, such as managing the operator log, acknowledging alarms, controlling the Keithley power supply, and archiving experiments, have been rebuilt as dedicated, highly responsive panels that remain functional even if the engine connection temporarily drops. The system now actively tracks experiment phases, automatically adapting the dashboard to display the most relevant data during cooldown, measurement, or warmup.

Delivering Predictive Analytics and Instrument Control
CryoDAQ now does more than just record data; it predicts the future of the experiment. We integrated predictive models for both vacuum and cooldown phases, providing operators with estimated times of arrival and confidence intervals directly on the dashboard. The sensor diagnostics engine continuously monitors instrument health, automatically escalating warnings and critical alarms through Telegram and the interface if anomalies persist. Furthermore, the Keithley power supply control has been refined with slew-rate limiting and strict compliance checks, while the calibration pipeline now supports continuous data collection and Chebyshev fitting. Four dedicated analytics widgets now display temperature trajectories, cooldown histories, and experiment summaries in real time.

Streamlining Lab Operations
To support continuous laboratory operations, we introduced an operator launcher that manages the engine and interface with automatic recovery, alongside a structured shift handover system. Experiment reporting is now automated to meet professional standards, and data archiving leverages efficient Parquet formats alongside traditional CSVs. A preflight checklist ensures systems are go before starting an experiment. With Telegram bot integration and a read-only web dashboard, operators can monitor their experiments remotely. Through systematic auditing and stabilization, CryoDAQ has matured into a reliable, intelligent partner for cryogenic research.
