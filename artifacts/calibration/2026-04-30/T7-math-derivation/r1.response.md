### Propagation of Uncertainty for Thermal Conductance Formula

#### 1. Formula for \( u(G) \)
The standard uncertainty in \( G \) is given by:
\[
u(G) = \frac{1}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2} \sqrt{ u(P)^2 (\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2 + P^2 \left[ u(\Delta T)^2 + u(\Delta T_0)^2 \right] }
\]

#### 2. Step-by-Step Derivation (GUM Methodology)
The thermal conductance is defined as:
\[
G = \frac{P}{\Delta T - \Delta T_0}
\]
where:
- \( P \): Heater power (uncertainty \( u(P) \))
- \( \Delta T \): Measured temperature difference (uncertainty \( u(\Delta T) \))
- \( \Delta T_0 \): Calibration offset (uncertainty \( u(\Delta T_0) \))

Assume \( P \), \( \Delta T \), and \( \Delta T_0 \) are **uncorrelated**. The uncertainty in \( G \) is propagated using the formula:
\[
u(G)^2 = \left( \frac{\partial G}{\partial P} \right)^2 u(P)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T} \right)^2 u(\Delta T)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T_0} \right)^2 u(\Delta T_0)^2
\]

**Step 1: Compute partial derivatives**  
- **Partial w.r.t. \( P \):**
  \[
  \frac{\partial G}{\partial P} = \frac{1}{\Delta T - \Delta T_0}
  \]
- **Partial w.r.t. \( \Delta T \):**
  \[
  \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T} = P \cdot (-1) \cdot (\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^{-2} = -\frac{P}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2}
  \]
- **Partial w.r.t. \( \Delta T_0 \):**
  \[
  \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T_0} = P \cdot (-1) \cdot (\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^{-2} \cdot (-1) = \frac{P}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2}
  \]

**Step 2: Substitute into uncertainty formula**  
\[
u(G)^2 = \left( \frac{1}{\Delta T - \Delta T_0} \right)^2 u(P)^2 + \left( -\frac{P}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2} \right)^2 u(\Delta T)^2 + \left( \frac{P}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2} \right)^2 u(\Delta T_0)^2
\]
Simplify squares:
\[
u(G)^2 = \frac{u(P)^2}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2} + \frac{P^2 u(\Delta T)^2}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^4} + \frac{P^2 u(\Delta T_0)^2}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^4}
\]
Combine terms with common denominator \( (\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^4 \):
\[
u(G)^2 = \frac{u(P)^2 (\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2 + P^2 \left[ u(\Delta T)^2 + u(\Delta T_0)^2 \right]}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^4}
\]
Take the square root to solve for \( u(G) \):
\[
u(G) = \frac{ \sqrt{ u(P)^2 (\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2 + P^2 \left[ u(\Delta T)^2 + u(\Delta T_0)^2 \right] } }{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2}
\]

#### 3. Relative Uncertainty \( \frac{u(G)}{|G|} \)
First, express \( G \) as:
\[
G = \frac{P}{\Delta T - \Delta T_0} \implies |G| = \left| \frac{P}{\Delta T - \Delta T_0} \right|
\]
Relative uncertainty squared:
\[
\left( \frac{u(G)}{|G|} \right)^2 = \frac{u(G)^2}{G^2} = u(G)^2 \cdot \frac{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2}{P^2}
\]
Substitute \( u(G)^2 \):
\[
\left( \frac{u(G)}{|G|} \right)^2 = \left[ \frac{ u(P)^2 (\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2 + P^2 \left( u(\Delta T)^2 + u(\Delta T_0)^2 \right) }{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^4} \right] \cdot \frac{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2}{P^2}
\]
Simplify:
\[
\left( \frac{u(G)}{|G|} \right)^2 = \frac{u(P)^2}{P^2} + \frac{u(\Delta T)^2 + u(\Delta T_0)^2}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2}
\]
Thus, the relative uncertainty is:
\[
\frac{u(G)}{|G|} = \sqrt{ \left( \frac{u(P)}{P} \right)^2 + \frac{u(\Delta T)^2 + u(\Delta T_0)^2}{(\Delta T - \Delta T_0)^2} }
\]

#### 4. Edge Cases
- **Near-zero denominator (\( \Delta T - \Delta T_0 \approx 0 \)):**  
  The uncertainty \( u(G) \) and relative uncertainty \( \frac{u(G)}{|G|} \) become extremely large (diverge to infinity). This makes \( G \) highly sensitive to input uncertainties when the net temperature difference is small. **Practical implication:** Avoid measurements where \( \Delta T \approx \Delta T_0 \).

- **Zero power (\( P = 0 \)):**  
  \( G = 0 \), but \( u(G) \) is undefined in the relative uncertainty formula. Use absolute uncertainty \( u(G) \) instead, which simplifies to \( \frac{u(P)}{|\Delta T - \Delta T_0|} \) (still problematic if \( \Delta T \approx \Delta T_0 \)).

#### 5. Discussion: Independence Assumption in Cryogenic Experiments
The assumption that \( \Delta T \) and \( \Delta T_0 \) are uncorrelated may **not hold** in cryogenic setups:
- **Shared reference sensor:** If the same temperature sensor is used to measure both \( \Delta T \) (under power) and \( \Delta T_0 \) (baseline), their uncertainties are correlated. For example, a systematic error in the sensor affects both.
- **Covariance term:** The general uncertainty formula should include a covariance term:
  \[
  u(G)^2 = \left( \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T} \right) \left( \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T_0} \right) u(\Delta T, \Delta T_0)
  \]
  where \( u(\Delta T, \Delta T_0) \) is the covariance. If \( \Delta T \) and \( \Delta T_0 \) are positively correlated, this term *reduces* \( u(G) \) because \( \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T} \) and \( \frac{\partial G}{\partial \Delta T_0} \) have opposite signs.  
- **Recommendation:** Characterize sensor correlations experimentally or via covariance estimation. If ignored, the uncertainty may be over/underestimated.

**Final Notes:**  
- **Absolute uncertainty:** Use \( u(G) \) for error margins.  
- **Relative uncertainty:** Use \( \frac{u(G)}{|G|} \) for fractional error analysis.  
- **Experimental design:** Maximize \( \Delta T - \Delta T_0 \) to minimize uncertainty amplification.
